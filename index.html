Skywalker's hideout tracked down

By Pizza The Hutt ✎ 12/31/99 ☢ 6 comments

Astonishing rumours have surfaced recently about Luke Skywalker's hideout. Now in his sixties, the iconic Jedi Knight played by Mark Hamill allegedly spends his time drinking blue bantha milk on a remote island in South Pacific, far from the intrigues and corruption poisoning the air in the big cities.

Aware of the new rise of evil, Skywalker has to solve a problem even more pressing than the next presidential US elections. But, as you already know, he's not in a hurry.

As part of his job, he has to stop Kylo Ren from becoming the next Darth Vader, after the parricide of Han Solo in episode VII. Apparently, that's not so hard to do: Ren is already psychotic and he failed to complete his training. As a consequence, Rey, the scavenger, beat the sh*t spit out of Kylo in the last scenes of the movie.

As long as Kylo's rough menace is not the problem, why would Skywalker hesitate to take on him? Ok, Kylo is Skywalker's nephew, as he is the son of Princess Leia, Skywalker's sister. Besides that, Kylo is the living proof of Skywalker's teaching techniquesâ€™ failure. Trained by the Jedi master, the nephew turned against his teacher and joined the Dork Dark Side.

Knowing all these things, we could agree that Kylo can't and mustn't be saved in the next series. The real challenge is to find someone convincing enough to deter Skywalker from trying to save him. We can't count on Leia for this matter: she's the monster's mother, for Force's sake! We can't even count on Rey - we'll soon find out that she and Kylo are, somehow, close relatives, as some of you have already guessed.

Will the salvation come from a yet unknown character? Unlikely, unless that new character is part of Skywalker's family as well. You see, if we learned something from the Star Wars series over the decades, is that the drama must be kept in the family.
